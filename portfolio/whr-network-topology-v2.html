Note: This is a revised architecture based on lessons learned from the first draft. The primary improvement is isolating VoIP phones and printers into dedicated VLANs, reducing the attack surface on the operations network.

Oasis at Wild Horse Ranch

Network Topology — Revised
v2 — Revised Architecture
What changed from v1: Due to the susceptible nature of VoIP and printer devices, they were moved to their own separate VLANs for isolation. Cross-communication needs to be allowed for office desktops and laptops to reach the printers for jobs, but should be limited otherwise. VoIP lines were isolated from other VLANs and given access to cloud servers only.
Environment: Indoor/outdoor wedding venue — office, event spaces, and grounds
Internet: Starlink → Cisco Meraki Go firewall → managed switch → wired and wireless endpoints
VLANs: 6 segments — Operations, Voice, Print, PCI, Staff Wi-Fi, Untrusted Wi-Fi
Cross-VLAN: Printing allowed from Operations and Staff Wi-Fi to Print VLAN via IPP/RAW ports
Guest Wi-Fi: Not offered — deliberate decision to minimize attack surface on a PCI-compliant network

DHCP ranges are intentionally narrow on Voice, Print, PCI, and AP Management VLANs to limit the number of devices that can obtain an address.

New in v2: VLAN 15 (Voice) and VLAN 18 (Print) are broken out from the original Operations VLAN. Phones and printers no longer share a broadcast domain with workstations.

PCI Note: Rules 1 and 2 must remain at the top. The card reader VLAN has no path to any internal resource.
New in v2: Rules 3–4 isolate the Voice VLAN. Rules 7–9 create a controlled print path: desktops and laptops can send print jobs to the printer, but the printer itself cannot initiate connections to anything. In v1, the printer sat on the same VLAN as desktops — a compromised printer had direct access to workstations.

Additional Security Measures

MeasureSettingRationale
DNS filteringOpenDNS (208.67.222.222, 208.67.220.220)GX20 forwards all DNS to OpenDNS — blocks malicious domains before they resolve. All VLANs forced to use GX20 as DNS server, preventing bypass.
Content filteringEnabled on VLAN 50 (Untrusted)Block malware, phishing, and adult content on the cell phone network
WPA3Enabled on Staff Wi-Fi and Untrusted Wi-Fi SSIDsStronger wireless encryption — prevents eavesdropping and offline dictionary attacks that WPA2 is vulnerable to
DHCP snoopingEnabled on managed switch, all VLANs — GX20 uplink port marked as trustedDrops rogue DHCP responses from untrusted ports. Builds a MAC-to-IP binding table used by ARP inspection.
Dynamic ARP inspectionEnabled on managed switch, all VLANs — requires DHCP snoopingValidates ARP packets against the DHCP snooping binding table. Prevents ARP spoofing / man-in-the-middle attacks on the LAN.
UPnPDisabledPrevents devices from automatically opening ports
Firewall managementCisco Meraki cloud app — no local admin interface exposedManaged via mobile app; no web UI or ports open for router administration
Remote desktopChrome Remote Desktop — no ports exposedEncrypted remote access for service maintenance; NAT traversal via Google relay, no open inbound ports
Firmware updatesAutomatic via Meraki cloudGX20 receives patches without manual intervention
Guest Wi-FiNot configuredDeliberate decision — reduces attack surface on a PCI-compliant network
